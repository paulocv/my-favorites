The **Boeing 777**, commonly referred to as the **Triple Seven**, is an American [long-range](/wiki/Long_haul) [wide-body airliner](/wiki/Wide-body_aircraft) developed and manufactured by [Boeing Commercial Airplanes](/wiki/Boeing_Commercial_Airplanes). The 777 is the world's largest [twinjet](/wiki/Twinjet) and the most-built wide-body airliner. The [jetliner](/wiki/Jet_airliner) was designed to bridge the gap between Boeing's other wide body airplanes, the twin-engined [767](/wiki/Boeing_767) and quad-engined [747](/wiki/Boeing_747), and to replace aging [DC-10](/wiki/DC-10) and [L-1011](/wiki/L-1011) [trijets](/wiki/Trijet). Developed in consultation with eight major airlines, the 777 program was launched in October 1990, with an order from [United Airlines](/wiki/United_Airlines). The [prototype aircraft](/wiki/B-HNL) rolled out in April 1994, and first flew that June. The 777 entered service with the launch operator United Airlines in June 1995. Longer-range variants were launched in 2000, and first delivered in 2004. Over 2,300 Boeing 777 aircraft have been ordered, with over 70 operators worldwide.
